## **Objective**
To help participants understand how prompt design influences AI outputs and to build hands-on skills for writing clear, structured, and effective prompts for real-world use.

---

## **Audience**
Students, professionals, and non-technical users exploring practical use of Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude.

---

## **Prerequisites**
- Access to at least one LLM (ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude)
- No programming knowledge required

---

## **1. Understanding Why Prompt Engineering Matters**

### **Area / Focus**
Understanding how different prompt formulations affect AI responses.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Asking general questions to AI systems  
- Using AI for explanations or learning support  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Generic or overly long responses  
- Lack of clarity or relevance  
- Unusable outputs for real needs  

### **Hands-On Exercise**
Run the following prompts and compare results:

Explain artificial intelligence.

Explain artificial intelligence to a non-technical audience  
in simple language using a real-world example.  
Limit the response to 5 bullet points.

### **Learning Insight**
Clear instructions improve relevance, tone, and usefulness.  
The model remains the same â€” the prompt controls the output.

---

## **2. Task Versatility of Modern LLMs**

### **Area / Focus**
Understanding how a single model performs multiple task types.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Content creation  
- Logical reasoning  
- Career or advisory responses  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Belief that AI is limited to one task  
- Confusion about how AI switches roles  

### **Hands-On Exercises**

Write a short story about a robot learning empathy.

A store sells pens at $2 each.  
If I buy 4 pens and get a $2 discount, how much do I pay?  
Explain step by step.

Act as a career coach and suggest 3 career paths  
for a computer science graduate.

### **Learning Insight**
LLMs are not task-specific tools.  
Instructions guide behavior, tone, and reasoning style.

---

## **3. How LLMs Generate Responses**

### **Area / Focus**
Understanding the predictive nature of language models.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Generating open-ended content  
- Brainstorming ideas  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Expecting identical outputs every time  
- Mistaking AI responses for fixed answers  

### **Hands-On Exercise**

Complete this sentence:  
Artificial intelligence will change the world by...

Run the prompt three times.

### **Learning Insight**
LLMs predict the most likely next words.  
Outputs are probabilistic, not memorized knowledge.

---

## **4. Creativity vs Consistency in AI Outputs**

### **Area / Focus**
Understanding randomness (creativity) in AI responses.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Idea generation  
- Innovation and brainstorming  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Safe but boring outputs  
- Overly wild or unfocused ideas  

### **Hands-On Exercises**

Give a startup idea related to education.

Give a highly creative and unconventional startup idea  
related to education.

### **Learning Insight**
More freedom in prompts leads to creativity.  
More constraints lead to consistency and structure.

---

## **5. Prompts as Instructions, Not Questions**

### **Area / Focus**
Reducing ambiguity by clearly specifying intent.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Summarizing documents  
- Extracting insights  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- AI guessing user intent  
- Irrelevant summaries  

### **Hands-On Exercises**

Summarize this article.

Summarize this article in 3 bullet points  
for a busy manager focused on decision-making.

### **Learning Insight**
Well-directed prompts reduce guesswork and improve focus.

---

## **6. Understanding What Counts as a Prompt**

### **Area / Focus**
Recognizing prompt components beyond text.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Writing emails  
- Drafting professional communication  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Inconsistent tone  
- Missing constraints  

### **Hands-On Exercises**

Write an email asking for a project deadline extension.

Write a polite and professional email asking for a  
3-day project deadline extension.  
The tone should be respectful and concise.

### **Learning Insight**
Role, tone, context, and constraints are all part of the prompt.

---

## **7. Iterative Prompt Improvement**

### **Area / Focus**
Learning to refine prompts step by step.

### **Typical Tasks**
- Explaining concepts  
- Training or educational content  

### **Pain Points / Challenges**
- Overly broad explanations  
- Mismatch with audience level  

### **Hands-On Exercise**

Explain cloud computing.

Improve the prompt by adding:
- Audience  
- Scope  
- Length  
- Output format  

Example improved prompt:

Explain cloud computing to college students  
using a real-life analogy.  
Limit the explanation to 150 words.

### **Learning Insight**
Effective prompting improves through iteration, not one attempt.

---

## **8. Structured Prompt Writing**

### **Area / Focus**
Using a simple and repeatable prompt structure.

### **Prompt Formula**
Role + Task + Context + Output

### **Hands-On Exercise**

Act as a technical writer.  
Summarize the following article for beginners.  
Use bullet points and keep it under 100 words.

Rewrite the same prompt for:
- Executives  
- Students  
- Developers  

---

## **9. Core Hands-On Practice Tasks**

### **Task 1: Rewrite Text**
Rewrite the following paragraph to sound more professional  
and concise. Keep the meaning unchanged.

### **Task 2: Summarization**
Summarize the following text in 5 bullet points.  
Focus on key ideas only.

### **Task 3: Information Extraction**
Extract dates, key people, and action items from the following text.  
Present the output in a table.

### **Task 4: Model Comparison**
Run the same prompt on ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude.

Explain machine learning to a 10-year-old.

Compare outputs based on:
- Tone  
- Simplicity  
- Accuracy  
- Creativity  

---

## **Learning Outcomes**

Participants will be able to:
- Understand how prompts guide AI behavior  
- Write clearer and more structured prompts  
- Predict how changes in prompts affect responses  
- Confidently apply LLMs to real-world tasks
